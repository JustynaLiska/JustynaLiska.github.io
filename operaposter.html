La Finta Giardiniera

RIAM - Opera Poster

Promotion & Identification

  • Client:

  • Aim:

  • Year:

    • RIAM
    Promotional Design for Opera
    2017

    The objective of this project was to create a promotional poster and information guide of the opera performed by Royal Irish Academy of Music. La Finta Giardiniera is an Italian soap opera written by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart at the age of eighteen. It contains a unique double mad scene that morphs into one of Mozart's signature ensemble finales. The main idea was to reflect this madness into a promotional piece of work.

    Defining audience in this particular project was challenging since the opera performed by RIAM characterise of attendance of every age group
    (well, maybe except small children). After getting
    some tips of the visual style of the performance and speaking with the opera hosts, I wanted to keep the visual consistancy, which in this case, was an 'old
    story told in a modern way'.

    I wanted to design something outstanding, disturbing and memorable, yet very modern. By creating the human hybrids, I aimed to introduce the 'dark' and 'crazy' atmosphere; I wanted to shock the older audience and surprise the younger one. That is why I have created a human-hybrid of a slug and frog*. Something disgusting and yet, something beautiful.

    Brochure:

    When it came to designing a brochure, I aimed for something which won't be thrown away as soon as the show is over. The outcome is a folded, double - side printed poster, with informational text at the back.
    In this case, the brochure can be treated like a piece
    of art, which can be hanged at home, or simply treated as a souvenir after a great show!

    The size of unfolded poster/brochure is slightly smaller than A2 when folded; it doesn't exceed A5 size. However, the first page differs in size, and this is due to the consistency of disturbed/glitched style
    I used in photography. For the material, it had to be a light paper easy to fold and yet, not too transparent. I have decided to use an 80gsm mat paper, which would be a great contrast to shiny, vibrant ink.
